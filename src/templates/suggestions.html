Exchange traded funds, or "ETFs" for short, are a great way to get diversification at a low cost. Because ETFs contain multiple stocks, they are a lot safer than buying individual stocks. For these reasons, our portfolio suggestions contain exclusively ETFs.

3 examples specific to a risk tolerance

  • Safe
    This simple portfolio gives you exposure to the whole stock market and is very safe for long term investing.
    ETF examples
    • - Globally diversified ETFs: VGRO and VEQT
  • Moderate
    This portfolio gives you exposure to the biggest companies in the world, those will give you good long term growth. It will also provide you with some good passive income, thanks to the REITS and dividend stocks ETFs.
    ETF examples
    • - S&P 500: VFV
    • - Technology: HXQ, TEC
    • - REITS: VRE, XRE, ZRE
    • - Dividend stocks: VDY, XEI
    • - Global exposure: XEF
    • - Aggregate bonds: VAB
    • - Federal bonds: VAB
  • Risky
    This portfolio is similar to the moderate risk portfolio, but it puts more emphasis on the SP500 (the 500 largest US companies) and on the technology sector, which has been the best performing sector of the last decade. This portfolio does not have bond exposure, so it is better suited for younger investors, or people with a relatively long investing horizon in front of them.
    ETF examples
    • - S&P 500: VFV
    • - Technology: HXQ, TEC
    • - REITS: VRE, XRE, ZRE
    • - Dividend stocks: VDY, XEI
    • - Global exposure: XEF
